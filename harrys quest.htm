“Harry’s Quest” Harry grew up in Wanganui, New Zealand. He was always very technically minded, and by the age of 14, he had began an apprenticeship working as a motor mechanic on Model T and Model A Ford cars. During this time, the amazement of aviation and its advances captured his imagination immensely. Since Smithy completed his historic 1928 Trans-Tasman flight with Charles Ulm, ‘Smithy’ held hero-like status around the world, and particularly to Harry. So, when Harry heard of Smithy's news to revisit New Zealand, he felt compelled to meet him. As the Southern Cross toured New Zealand in 1934, Harry (now aged 19) did whatever he could do to be at their next destination, and to the great surprise to Smithy & the crew, he kept showing up. He walked great distances, hitch hiked and slept wild to be at their next destination. Occasionally he would miss them, so he would immediately set out for their next destination. Smithy was so impressed with Harry's efforts, that he started giving him various tasks working on the Southern Cross itself. Tommy Pethybridge offered him 1 pound for his efforts, and although broke, Harry firmly refused the offer. They later let him sleep in the Southern Cross' cabin for a couple of nights. To Harry’s sheer delight, they even invited him in the air with them, for their last leg of their tour to Wigram Aerodrome. Before Smithy left for Australia, he made Harry a fantastic offer of offering him work if he could get himself to Australia. Smithy own words were, "It is out of the question to consider taking Sergent to Australia with the plane, for I shall be carrying mail and may have to send some of my regular crew across by ship. But this lad deserves consideration, and I shall certainly give him a position for the present, and will consider him later, when the air service in New Zealand comes into being". After Smithy and his crew departed for Sydney, Harry made every attempt to get himself to Smithy's business at Mascot, Sydney. He attempted offering to work his way to Sydney aboard various ships, but was frequently turned down. So he stowed away aboard the Monteray, but he was caught and returned to New Zealand. He then decided to return to Wanganui to work and save, and a few months later, in January 1935, Harry sailed for Sydney. When he arrived in Sydney, Smithy employed him as Ground Staff / Engineer at his Mascot Aerodrome. Harry also soon went on to obtain his pilot's licence there a year later.
