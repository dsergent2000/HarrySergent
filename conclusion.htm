“Conclusion” In 1952, Harry left B.O.A.C. and returned to Australia. At this stage he did not have the same interest in aviation as in the early years, because it was beginning to involve many areas of specialisation. He preferred working on earlier models of aircraft, as the engineers then were often involved in all aspects of their maintenance. Now base in Australia, he ultimately established "W.I.S. Fluxes", a business specialising in the development and manufacturing of fluxes for the gas welding industry. He remained a keen member of the Australian Early Birds (a group that were involved in aviation prior to WW2). He has a collection of all their meetings’ newsletters, and he happily attended his last meeting with lifelong friend, Bruce Cowan. Harry had 3 sons with Renee: Clive, Brian & Allan Sergent. He later was re-married, to Daphne, with whom he spent the last 35 years of his life, based in the Penrith area of Sydney. Towards his retirement, he had a long running issue with the proposed Badgery's Creek airport. It This unfortunately caused him a lot of stress, and was damaging towards his retirement, due to the issue of selling his 25 acre property. ‘Harry’ was a socialist, atheist, and was strictly opposed to racism. He lived by his motto, "Evil flourishes in the world, when good people stand by and do nothing". Harry is buried next to his wife Daphne, his tombstone reads "His Spirit Now Flies Free".
